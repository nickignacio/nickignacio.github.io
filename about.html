About Me

I grew up in Germany and Texas and received my S.B. in Materials Science and Engineering from MIT. As an undergrad I was a teaching assistant, studied abroad, and was vice president of the Society of Undergraduate Materials Scientists as a Senior.

My enthusiasm for materials research has led me to pursue a Ph.D. in Materials Science and Engineering. Aside from spending my time sharing my love for materials, I enjoy trying new recipies, endurance sports, playing games, and falling on Judo mats.

Personal Interests

Materials Science Pedagogy and Outreach

Materials science pedagogy is a career path I'd like to pursue post graduate school. I've continued as an assistant instructor and teaching assistant for classes at UT after leading a recitation section for MIT's 3.091 taught by Prof. Jeff Grossman. I am taking classes under Prof. Maura Borrego in pursuit of the Engineering Education Certificate granted by the graduate school of engineering.


Additionally, outreach is important to me as an extension of my teaching passion. I volunteer with UT's Materials Research Sciecnce and Engineering Centers (MRSEC) funded by NSF to lead STEM K-12 events at local schools in Austin
