About

I'm an MS/PhD Student in the Texas Materials Institute at the Universtiy of Texas at Austin.
I fabricate 2D Material based Phase Change Memory, and study them with electron microscopy. I'm passionate about teaching and DEI efforts in education.

Moved to Austin and started my PhD

July, 2021

After graduating MIT and leaving Formlabs, I made the drive down from Cambridge to Austin where I started my PhD at UT Austin. I'm in the Texas Materials Insitute as a Cockrell School of Engineering fellow advised by Professor Deji Akinwande. I study 2D materials for non-von Neumann memory devices including RRAM and PCM using electron microscopy techniques such as STM and TEM.

Nylon 11 released and leaving formlabs

June 11, 2021

I'm glad over 8 months of my time at Formlabs resulted in a product being released in Nylon 11! Working with the best guidance from my manager and alongside the SLS materials team, it was extremely satisfying to take a material from an R&D stage to release.


Formlabs was an incredible environment to spend the last year of undergrad and filled with the most passionate and creative people I've met. I took a lot more from working there than just some fun 3D printed desk decorations.

Started research in Grossman group

June, 2020

I knew I wanted to go to grad school so with research plans in Oxford and an internship for the summer of 2020 at Formlabs cancelled, I joined Professor Grossman's research group for the summer. I was able to design a small research project studying the effect of different dopants on diffusion paths for nanoparticle catalysts using DFT. Throughout the summer I was able to develop independent research skills and get some computational research experience in what was my first taste of independent research.
